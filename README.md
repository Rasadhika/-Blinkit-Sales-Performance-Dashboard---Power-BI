# -Blinkit-Sales-Performance-Dashboard---Power-BI
Overview
This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of Blinkit's sales performance, customer satisfaction, and inventory distribution. It is designed to surface key business insights and highlight opportunities for optimization across products, outlets, and geographies.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

KPI                   Description

Total Sales:          Overall revenue generated from all items sold

Average Sales:        Average revenue per sale

Number of Items:      Total count of distinct items sold

Average Rating:       Average customer rating across items sold

Dashboard Sections
1. Total Sales by Fat Content
Analyzes how fat content (Low Fat vs Regular) impacts total sales, alongside Average Sales, Number of Items, and Average Rating.

2. Total Sales by Item Type
Breaks down sales performance across different product categories to identify top and bottom performers.

3. Fat Content by Outlet (Total Sales)
Compares total sales across outlets, segmented by fat content — useful for understanding product preferences per location.

4. Total Sales by Outlet Establishment
Evaluates how the age or type of an outlet establishment influences its sales performance over time.

5. Sales by Outlet Size
Examines the correlation between outlet size (Small / Medium / Large) and total sales contribution as a percentage.

6. Sales by Outlet Location
Visualizes the geographic distribution of sales across Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 locations.

7. All Metrics by Outlet Type
A consolidated view of all four KPIs (Total Sales, Average Sales, Number of Items, Average Rating) broken down by outlet type (e.g., Grocery Store, Supermarket).

Key Findings

Overall performance: The business generated $1.20M in total sales across 8,523 items, with an average sale value of $141 and an average customer rating of 3.9 / 5.

Fat content drives majority of sales: Low fat products account for 64.73% of sales compared to 35.27% for regular fat — suggesting customers lean toward perceived healthier options.

Medium outlets dominate: Medium-sized outlets lead with 42.27% ($507.90K) of total sales, followed by Small at 37.01% ($444.79K) and High/Large at 20.72% ($248.99K). Smaller and mid-size outlets are clearly the revenue backbone.

2018 was the peak year: Outlets established in 2018 recorded the highest sales at $205K, indicating that year was either a high-growth period or saw the most successful outlet openings.

Fruits & Vegetables top the product list: With 1,232 items, Fruits and Vegetables is the largest category by item count — reflecting Blinkit's core grocery identity.

Seafood is the smallest category: With only 64 items, Seafood has the lowest representation, which 
could point to either low demand or a deliberate limited assortment strategy.
